Onboarding Dashboard

During orientation I kept hearing the same issues from new hires around equipment not arriving, system access not being set up, and scheduling conflicts. I built a unified dashboard connecting the heads of IT, L&D, HR, and Finance to fix the root problem. Maintained every afternoon with real-time updates. In our Agile environment, any rescind, start date change, or candidate drop was pushed to all departments immediately with confirmation required. Day 1 issues dropped to zero within two weeks.

Exit Interview Program

I noticed the same names cycling through resignations and new reqs on the sales team. I volunteered to run exit interviews, identified the root causes, documented everything, and brought a full summary to the VP of HR and Director of Recruiting. The program now directly shapes the company's sales retention strategy.

Requisition Creation and Governance

My Approach

How I Work.

Before I Source
Understand
  • Run a structured intake with every hiring manager before touching the market
  • Define what great actually looks like for this specific team and this specific moment
  • Research the talent landscape and compensation benchmarks before posting
  • Set honest expectations on timeline, availability, and tradeoffs upfront
  • Identify the real blockers before they become offer killers
While I Search
Stay Connected
  • Check in with both the candidate and hiring manager every few days throughout the process
  • Build outbound passive pipelines using LinkedIn Recruiter and Boolean search
  • Report weekly on pipeline health, conversion rates, and market data
  • Surface concerns early before they become surprises at the offer stage
  • Maintain data integrity in Dayforce across all active searches at all times
After the Close
Follow Through
  • Walk every finalist through the offer before it lands in their inbox
  • Check in with both the new hire and hiring manager at week one
  • Follow up again at 30, 60, and 90 days to confirm the transition is on track
  • Surface any early concerns before they become retention problems
  • The search is not done until they stay
