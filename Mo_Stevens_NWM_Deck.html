02 — Sourcing Strategy

Where I find the 13.

Advisor recruiting requires multi-channel sourcing. People are not searching for this career. You have to meet them where they already are.

Digital channels

  • LinkedIn Recruiter: passive outreach, boolean search, career change signals
  • Indeed and Handshake: active candidates and new grads open to financial careers
  • Facebook Groups: Milwaukee professional and community groups
  • Social media recruiting: NM brand promotion through organic content and events

Community channels

  • Professional organizations: UWM alumni, SHRM Milwaukee, local business networks
  • Career events: campus recruiting, community job fairs, industry nights
  • Referral network: ask every advisor and leader who they would want to work with again
  • Community advocates: build relationships with people who know people

Outreach approach

  • Personalized direct messages, not templates
  • Lead with the opportunity, not the company name
  • 3-4 day follow up cadence, no candidate goes cold
  • Every touchpoint is consistent and clear on next steps

Referral engine

  • Ask every advisor: who would you want on this team?
  • Referral candidates convert faster and stay longer
  • Build referral sources before roles become urgent
  • Track referral conversion separately to measure quality

03 — How I Work

Sourcing and keeping people in the process.

Finding someone is the first step. Keeping them engaged through a career change conversation is the actual work.

🔍

Sourcing

I build pipelines before roles become urgent. LinkedIn Recruiter, Indeed, Handshake, referrals, direct outreach, community events. I look for people who are not actively looking and start conversations that feel worth having.

📩

Touch Points

I follow up with every candidate every 3 to 4 days throughout the process. No one wonders where they stand. Candidates who feel informed stay in the process. Candidates who go quiet usually do not come back.

🌐

Referral Networks

I ask every advisor and leader who they would want to work alongside. Referral candidates convert faster and stay longer. Building that network is an ongoing habit, not a one-time ask.

🤝

Independent

I manage my own pipeline, prioritize my own outreach, and flag issues before they become problems. I do not need a lot of hand-holding to stay productive. I know what the goal is and I work toward it.
